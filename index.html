Ceara

Ceará is a state in the northeastern Brazil, characterized by having a summer for all year. It is characterized by a tropical climate, having a coastline of 573km, the third longest in Northeast region. In the economy, it stands out for the production of leather and derived products, a raw material also used in handicrafts. The state is currently the third in wind energy production in Brazil. The tourism is an activity of fundamental importance for the state economy. Ceará receives more than 2 million tourists annually.
